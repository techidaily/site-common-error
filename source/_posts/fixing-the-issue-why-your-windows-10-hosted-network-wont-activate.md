### Fix 1: Try the Startup Repair

 When your computer won’t boot, the first fix is always booting your computer into the recovery environment (RE) and perform the Startup Repair. Here’s how:

1. Ensure your PC is off.
2. Press the power button to turn on your PC, then hold the power button down until PC shuts down automatically (about 5 seconds).
3. Repeat this more than 2 times until you see the Preparing Automatic Repair.  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/03/img_56ebc90860567.png)  
**Note** : This step aims to bring up the**Preparing Automatic Repair screen** . If you’ve seen this screen for the first time when you power up the computer, skip this step.

### Fix 3: Use Command Prompt

 The broken system files may relate to this issue. If so, you could run Command Prompt to check and fix the problem.  
 The first few steps are the same as[Fix 1](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/) .

1. When you see**Advanced options** , click**System Restore** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/cmd-1.jpg)
2. Choose the account, type password and click**Continue** .
3. Type**chkdsk/f** to fix errors on the disk.  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/f.jpg)
4. After this process, reboot your computer to check.

### Fix 4: Perform a system restore

 This problem could be fixed by performing a system restore. If your computer won’t boot after the update, then reverse to the previous system logically should fix this issue.  
 Please note, system restore only affect recently installed programs and drivers.

 The first few steps are the same as[Fix 1](https://tools.techidaily.com/drivereasy/download/) .

1. When you see**Advanced options** , click**System Restore** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/restore.jpg)
2. Choose the account, type password and click**Continue** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/continue.jpg)
3. Choose a restore point.
