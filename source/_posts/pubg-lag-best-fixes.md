## Fix 2: Clear download cache

 Sometimes corrupted cache data can cause download problems. So you can try to clear the download cache to get rid of any obsolete files impeding download progress:

1. Open the **Steam**  menu and select **Settings** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/steam-settings.jpg)
2. Click**Downloads** . Then select **CLEAR DOWNLOAD CACHE** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/steam-clear-download-cache.jpg)
3. Click**OK** in the pop-up box.  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/steam-clear-download-cache-ok.jpg)

 You can resume the download to check again. If the issue disappears, congrats! If not, just move on to the next fix.

## Fix 6: Flush DNS cache

 An obsolete DNS resolver cache in your computer could also prevent your Steam from connecting to download servers, and deleting it may help. Here’s how to do it:

1. Click the **Start** button, and type **cmd** in the Windows search bar. Then right-click**Command Prompt** and select **Run as administrator** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/cmd-command-prompt-modified-1.jpg)
2. When a pop-up box comes up, type**ipconfig /flushdns** . Then hit**enter** key. You should see a message which confirms that the DNS Resolver Cache has been flushed successfully.

## Fix 8: Temporarily disable your antivirus software

 Your firewall or antivirus software can sometimes cause interference to the network connection of your Steam client.

 To see if that happens, temporarily disable your antivirus program for the duration of the Steam download and see if the issue persists. (You can consult your antivirus program documentation for instructions on disabling it.)

 If this resolves your problem, you can add Steam to the whitelist of your firewall by visiting its configuration pane. You can also contact the vendor of your firewall and ask for help.

 When your antivirus service is disabled, be careful about what sites you visit, what emails you open and what files you download.
