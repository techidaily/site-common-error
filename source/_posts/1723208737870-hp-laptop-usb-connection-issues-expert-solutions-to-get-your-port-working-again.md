### Fix 1: Disconnect ALL USB devices and re-seat your battery

 Sometimes this**USB ports not working** problem is only a hiccup that can be easily solved by removing all the peripherals and reseating the battery before getting your HP laptop back to where it is.

Here’s how:

1. Remove**ALL** the USB devices from your HP laptop.
2. Power off your laptop and disconnect it from power supplies.
3. Carefully remove the battery from the back of the laptop. (You might also need to check the manual if you’re unsure how this is done).
4. Wait 10 minutes before you seat the battery back in.
5. Insert**ALL** the USB devices and recharge your PC.
6. Restart your PC.
7. Check the USB ports to see if the issue has been resolved. If yes, then great! If it still remains, please move on to**Fix 2** , below.

---

### Fix 2:  Change power management settings for USB Root Hubs

Tweaking the properties in **USB Root Hub**  devices is another proven effective way to solve our**USB ports not working on HP laptop** issue. To do this:

1. On the keyboard of the target computer, press **the Windows logo key** and **R**  at the same time, then copy & paste **devmgmt.msc** into the box and click **OK** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/img_5afb9c1b96ba9.png)
2. Double-click on **Universal Serial Bus controllers**   \>  **the first**   item of **USB Root Hub** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/img_5b72a8b859168.jpg)
3. CLICK **the box**   before   **Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power**  whether it’s checked or unchecked. Then click **OK** .
