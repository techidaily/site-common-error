## 1\. For starters, if you‚Äôre experiencing a sudden FPS drop‚Ä¶

 If the FPS drop you‚Äôre experiencing happens all of a sudden, and the problem wasn‚Äôt seen before at all, then the problem could be with the network connection situation. In this case, you need to try the following first to make sure that your network connection is still all good and with no interrupts:

* **Check the game status page** . You can usually find the status page by searching ‚Äúgame name + status‚Äù on Google. If the game servers are interrupted, then the sudden FPS drop should be a universal problem, so all you can do is wait for the game developers to get their servers fixed.
* **Use a wired network connection** (with an Ethernet cable) instead of Wi-Fi. If this is not an option, move your computer closer to the router.
* **Play on your local server** . If that‚Äôs not an option, choose one that is closest to you.
* **Update your router firmware** if you haven‚Äôt already.
* **Don‚Äôt use any VPN, proxy or antivirus programs or services** , as they might also interrupt your network, and cause the sudden FPS dropping issue.

 If the FPS never looks good on your computer during games, please move on to check other fixes.

## 4\. Close bandwidth-intensive services and applications

 If there are bandwidth-intensive services and applications, like background downloads, music streaming, or video streaming, running when you‚Äôre gaming, please disable them, as their occupation of the network connection resource is likely the culprit to your low FPS in games.

To do so:

1. Right-click the Windows taskbar and select **Task Manager** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/image-48.png)
2. Select each resource-hogging application and click**End Task** to close them one by one.  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/image-49.png)

 Then run your game again and see if the low FPS issues are fixed. If the problem still remains, please move on to the next fix below.

## 7\. Change the Power Mode

 The default power plan by Windows strikes a balance between power consumption and PC performance, which is a good choice most of the time, especially when you‚Äôre not using resource-hungry applications a lot. But games usually need more resources than other regular software programs, so it‚Äôs not a bad idea to switch to the**High performance** plan to improve your game performance. This is even more true when you‚Äôre having a low FPS.

To change the power mode:

1. On your keyboard, press **the Windows logo key**  and **R** at the same time, then type **powercfg.cpl** and press **Enter** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/run-dialog-powercfg.png)
2. In the pop-up window, expand **Hide additional plans** and select **High performance** .  
![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/control-panel-power-options.png)
3. Then run your game to see if the frame rate is higher. If the problem remains still, please move on to the next fix.

## 8\. Defragment your hard drive (HDD only)

 It‚Äôs usually suggested that you install the games (especially newly released ones in recent years) on your SSD rather than HDD. This is because games usually consume more hardware resources than the other programs, and installing the game on the SSD is one of the basics to make sure that your games run smoothly, given the more advanced hardware support.

 If you don‚Äôt have an SSD, and you have a low FPS, it‚Äôs probably time for you to defragment your HDD, especially when you haven‚Äôt done it in a rather long time. To do so:

 Please don‚Äôt defragment your SSD, as it could shorten the life expectancy of your hard drive.

1. On your keyboard, press the**Windows** key and type**defrag** . Then select**Defragmenet and Optimize Drives** from the list.  

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/image-109.png)
2. Select the hard drive that you have installed the game on, and select**Optimize** .  

![](https://images.drivereasy.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/image-110.png)
3. Then run your game again to see if the frame rate remains low. If so, please move on.
